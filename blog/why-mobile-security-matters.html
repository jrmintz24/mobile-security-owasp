Why Mobile Security Matters (Even to Sales Guys Like Me)

I’m not an engineer. I can’t reverse malware or dissect encryption protocols. I’m a sales guy. I talk to people, ask questions, tell stories — and sell things that make security teams' lives easier.

So when I joined a mobile security company, I asked the obvious question: “Wait... people actually attack mobile apps?”

The short answer? Yes. The long answer? You have no idea how deep the rabbit hole goes — and neither did I.

Over the last few months, I’ve watched demos, talked to CISOs, and read more OWASP than I care to admit. I’ve learned that mobile apps are often the least protected part of a company’s digital presence — even though they hold customer data, control hardware, and connect to critical APIs.

But here’s the thing that really stuck with me: attackers don’t care whether a target is 'mobile' or not. They care whether it’s vulnerable. And most apps are.

Now I’m not going to pretend I’ve become a mobile security expert overnight. What I can do is bring a human lens to it. I can share the real conversations I’m having with prospects, the objections I hear, and how I explain MTD, app shielding, and jailbreak detection without sounding like a SOC analyst.

And yes, I use AI to help me do it — including this post. That’s the point. This blog isn’t just about mobile threats. It’s about how someone like me — non-technical but curious — can learn to care, contribute, and educate others in the process.

So if you're in sales, marketing, or just trying to talk tech without buzzword bingo, this blog’s for you. Let’s learn together. And let’s make mobile safe — for real humans.

— Randy (with a little help from ChatGPT)
